Theme: The description of the national rural planning system  
(The concrete topic could be: Rural Planning System in China/India/the Netherlands, etc.)   
“Rural planning aims to bring about economic development yet maintain the sustainability of 
renewable resources and reduce income disparities. The goals of rural planning varied depending 
on the political ideology of nation states that formed the first, second, and third worlds. Forces of 
globalization during recent decades have erased many of the international differences in rural 
planning. The current global challenge of rural planning is to build up and diversify the rural 
economy, reduce inequity, and exploit the land and natural resources in a way that allows renewal 
and is sustainable.” 
Hemalata C. Dandekar, in International Encyclopedia of the Social & Behavioral Sciences (Second 
Edition), 2015 
With the previous in mind, the core task of this assignment is to describe and critically reflect 
upon the rural planning system in a selected spatial setting (country). Students are free to 
choose any case study (the selected rural planning system of a specific country) on their own. 
Main tasks: 
1. Find appropriate primary (international and national policies, laws, spatial strategies, spatial 
plans, land-use plans, etc.) and secondary sources (journal articles, chapters, books, etc.) that 
describe the selected case study, i.e., the selected rural planning system of a specific country. 
2. By screening the literature, elucidate (all or some) of the following: – the embeddedness of a rural planning system within the broader setting (social system, 
political and economic features, dominant governance model) – significant changes in the rural policies over time (if any) – dominant approach to rural planning (see Lecture 2) – particular aspects (economic, social, environmental) highlighted in the rural policy – relevant institutional structure in charge of rural planning – important regulatory instruments (policies, strategies, plans) on rural planning – impact of global challenges on the evolution of rural policy  – current challenges of implementing the rural policy 
The task is a 6-minute group presentation (PowerPoint or similar format), i.e., an independent 
group study of 6 students per group, on the above topic. The presentation is scheduled for week 11 
(12/13.05.2026). Note that all the groups should upload their presentations by 12.05.2026. The 
class presentation is scheduled as follows: groups 1-10 will present on 12.05.2026, while groups 
11-20 will present on 13.05.2026.
